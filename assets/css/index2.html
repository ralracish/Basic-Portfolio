About Me

Hi. I am 45 years old. I was born in Thomasville, GA and was raised most of my childhood in Newton Grove, NC. I graduated valedictorian of my high school class. I was a Tarheel basketball fan and fell in love with the campus at UNC when I visited in high school. I was the first person in my extended family to go to college. I only applied to UNC and began attending college there in 1991. I studied abroad in Mexico City for a semester in 1993. While in school, I found a job as a Grants and Contracts Administrator at the medical school at UNC. I started working there full time and continued my education part time. I graduated with a BA in International Studies with a concentration in Political Science and Latin America in 1997. At this point, I was also a single mom.

From that position, I also became Executive Director of an international medical research membership organization, and then Administrative Director of a nonprofit that provided education and resources in the field of functional GI disorders. In these positions, I traveled all over the US and internationally to places like Toronto (twice), Rome (several times), Vienna (twice), London, and Lucerne. I have also done accounting work for a domestic violence shelter and a CPA office, and then worked again as a Grants and Contracts Administrator at Duke University. Now I am taking the UNC Coding Bootcamp Course to become a full stack developer and spend the next stage of my life in a career in the IT field.

I enjoy reading, watching streaming videos (right now I'm binge-watching "The Americans" on Amazon Prime), and playing scrabble. I have a special love for cats and have a 4 yr old male gray tabby named Tigger, and a 7 mo old female gray tabby named Bella.
